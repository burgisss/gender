WHAT IS GENDER AND DEVELOPMENT (GAD)?

Gender and development is a field of study and practice that focuses on understanding and addressing the ways in which gender inequalities intersect with development processes. It emphasizes the importance of gender analysis and mainstreaming in all aspects of development work, aiming to promote gender equality and empower individuals of all genders to participate fully in and benefit from development initiatives.

In addition to promoting gender equality, gender and development initiatives often seek to challenge traditional gender roles and norms that contribute to discrimination and marginalization. This includes advocating for policy reforms, implementing gender-responsive programs and projects, and fostering gender-sensitive attitudes and behaviors within communities and institutions. By integrating gender perspectives into development planning and implementation, this approach aims to create more inclusive and sustainable outcomes that address the diverse needs and experiences of people of all genders. Furthermore, gender and development efforts recognize the interconnectedness of gender with other axes of identity, such as class, race, ethnicity, and sexuality, highlighting the importance of adopting an intersectional approach to effectively address social inequalities and promote social justice.
